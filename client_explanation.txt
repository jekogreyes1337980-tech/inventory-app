INVENTORY SYSTEM UPGRADE: WHAT'S NEW (Client Presentation Guide)
================================================================

We have completed a major structural upgrade to the inventory system. Here is a simple, non-technical breakdown of the improvements we have made and what they mean for the business.

---

1. REPLACING THE "OFFLINE" DATABASE WITH A REAL SERVER
------------------------------------------------------
* What it was: Previously, all system data (inventory counts, orders, logs) was stored only in the browser's temporary memory (local storage). If someone cleared their browser cache or used a different computer, the data was gone.
* What it is now: We have migrated to a professional backend server (Node.js) and a real database (SQLite). 
* Why this matters:
  - Reliability: Data is now securely saved in a permanent database file.
  - Multi-user support: Multiple employees (Administrators, Stock Room Staff, and Storefront Staff) can now access, edit, and share the exact same inventory logs in real time from different devices.

---

2. IMPROVING SYSTEM PERFORMANCE AND SECURITY
--------------------------------------------
* What we did: We took the entire application and cleaned up the internal code, breaking it down into organized modules (database handling, screen interfaces, and button actions).
* Why this matters: 
  - Stability: The system runs smoother and loads faster.
  - Easier updates: It is much simpler to add new features or troubleshoot specific parts of the app without affecting the rest of the program.

---

3. ROADMAP: WHAT WE ARE BUILDING NEXT
--------------------------------------
With the database server now in place, we are set up to implement the next major features:

* Real Login Accounts:
  We will replace the temporary "Simulate Role" dropdown with secure, personal user logins. Employees will log in with their own credentials, and the system will automatically show their allowed views.

* Staff Assignment & Responsibility Tracking:
  Administrators will have the sole power to assign specific tickets (Stock In deliveries or Stock Out orders) to selected staff members. 

* Prevention of Editing Conflicts (Locking):
  To eliminate human errors and double-entry mistakes, the system will lock a ticket while a staff member is actively working on it. This ensures only one person is responsible for the count and status of that ticket at any given time.
